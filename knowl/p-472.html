Solving for \(T_a^4 \) yields \(T^4_a = \frac{Q}{\sigma(2-\epsilon_a)}\) and
\begin{align*} T^4_a = \frac{Q}{\sigma(2-\epsilon_a)} \phantom{a} \amp or \amp \phantom{a} T_a = \left(\frac{Q}{\sigma(2-\epsilon_a)}\right)^{\frac{1}{4}} \phantom{a} K \\ \amp \amp \\ T^4_E = \frac{2Q}{\sigma(2-\epsilon_a)} \phantom{a} \amp or \amp \phantom{a} T_E = \left(\frac{2Q}{\sigma(2-\epsilon_a)}\right)^{\frac{1}{4}} \phantom{a} K \end{align*}
Let's plug in the values for the constants.
  • \(\displaystyle Q = 239.2 W ^*\)
  • \(\displaystyle \sigma = 5.67 \times 10^{-8} {W\,m^{-2}\,K^{-4}} \)
\(^*\)Note: (\(Q = \frac{S}{4}(1-\alpha)\) with the solar constant \(S = 1367 Wm^{-2} \) and albedo \(\alpha = 0.3\))
