Inform students that there is a faster way to decrypt shift ciphers than saying the alphabet backwards in their heads. Ask them to try decrypting a message that was encrypted with a very large forward shift such as \(+25\text{.}\) How would they do it? Likely by shifting each letter forwards by \(1\) to obtain the plaintext message. This means that in the English alphabet, \(0=25+1=26\text{,}\) and now you can introduce modular arithmetic (working with modulus \(26\) for the foreseeable future).
